Unite 2024 Bible Seminar 2 - The church is preaching and prayerful (Acts 4:1-31)

Theme: Christ continues to build his church through the bold proclamation of his gospel in spite of opposition.
Aim: Boldly speak the gospel even when opposition comes and pray for God’s help in doing this.

Notes

If we are following Jesus the chances are that we will know that speaking for Christ makes life harder. Speaking for Christ may bring awkwardness, opposition, hostility, ridicule and stress. These may even be  the reasons stopping us from becoming Christian. Nevertheless, from it’s very first days the church has grown through speaking boldly about Jesus.

The church speaks boldly: because it works (1-4)

Peter has healed a man ‘in Jesus’ name' (Acts 3:1-9), this presented him with the opportunity to preach (Acts 3:11-26). Peter’s confronts Jesus’ (unwitting) murders with their need to repent (3:19,26). In verses 1-4 we see two different reactions to this, the first is annoyance and vexation (4:2). The priests, the captain of the temple guard (High priests right hand man) and Sadducees (largest group of ruling Jews) object to the message that Peter is preaching. Peter’s message has gone beyond saying that Jesus was resurrected into saying that through him others will be resurrected. The Sadducees naturally oppose this because they didn’t believe there was a resurrection. The others were probably more worried that Peter would cause a disturbance that would cause the Romans to clamp down on Jerusalem further or would upset their position of authority with the people. However, Luke wants us to know that speaking boldly has been effective, the second response is that 5000 men believed the word, presumably more including women. This is why the Jewish leaders act so strongly.
Potential application: What do we think will make our friends Christians? Speaking the gospel might not look impressive but is God’s way of growing his church.

The church speaks boldly: because Jesus alone works (5-12)

It is ominous that the rulers, elders, scribes and High Priest, the Sanhedrin, gather together in Jerusalem, this is what the kings of the earth do in verse 26. Caiaphas was the High Priest presiding over Jesus’ arrest and execution. Peter had cowered outside Jesus’ trial and denied Christ, however at his own trial the Holy Spirit helps him of speak boldly about Christ (4:8,13). The charge is essentially, ‘who gave you authority to do this?’ (4:7), Peter’s answer is Jesus. Jesus work of salvation is ongoing as this mans healing has shown, in verse 9 the word for healing is actually saved. Salvation and resurrection and healing are all of one piece, the lame man leaping has been given as a small foretaste of resurrection life, the messianic age has arrived (Isaiah 35:6). 
Psalm 118 foreshadowed what the Jewish leaders had done. In Psalm 118 the king has been rejected by the people of God and thrown out of the city but is vindicated by God and so returns in triumph to be recognised ruler of God’s kingdom, this is precisely what had happened to Jesus. Jesus’ message is wonderfully inclusive, it’s for absolutely everyone, even his enemies, but also very exclusive, salvation can be found nowhere else because no one else has died for your sins. There are no other paths to God.
Potential application: What else do people look to to save them? Will anything else work? Getting top grades in exams will not save you because they do nothing to your sins.

The church speaks boldly: because opposition doesn’t work (13-22)

The Sanhedrin were the powerful ruling council of Jerusalem and yet come across as a bit of a joke. Their charge in 4:7 reveals their ignorance, arrogance and fear. They put Peter & John on trial for an act of kindness,  (4:9 cf. Luke 6:9-11 doing good). Even uneducated men seem to know more about God than they do (4:13) and more willing to listen to Him (4:19). It is important to notice that they have basically no arguments against the gospel. This would be the perfect opportunity to point out how wrong Peter is about the resurrection but they can neither disprove what has happened nor its significance. Their only option is to try to intimidate Peter & John into silence, which they can’t because their authority is trumped by God (4:19). To crown it all the rulers live in fear of the people they are supposed to lead (4:21) .
Further to this, in verse 25-28 the point is that those who oppose God are on the wrong side of history. In Psalm 2:4-6 God laughs at those who oppose him as he establishes his king (Jesus) in Zion. He is not threatened by opposition, it would be a hilarious joke were it not that he takes it very seriously and ‘rebuke them in his anger and terrifies them in his wrath’ (Psalm 2:5). Opposition only succeeds in furthering God’s plans as is demonstrated at the cross. (4:28)
Potential application: When it comes to the choice of listening to God or to people who do you listen to and why? It should be no contest but rarely is.

The church speaks boldly: because prayer works (23-27)

Peter and John are released and return to their friends. Prayer is the churches instinctive response as they hear what has happened. Firstly their prayer acknowledges who God is; he is the one who spoke heaven and earth into being and the one who is never caught off guard by opposition but powerful works it for his own good purposes. It is not wrong to pray for opposition to end but I suspect that the church didn’t think that was the priority, God’s kingdom even can grow because of opposition. Instead the church prays for bold speech in the face of opposition, a prayer this is answered immediately (4:31). Things shaking are generally a sign of God’s presence. God’s people already have the Spirit but in this particular instance He helps them to speak boldly.
Potential application: How do we respond to opposition? Is our response prayerful and faithful that God has it under control? Do we pray for opposition to stop or for more boldness to speak?
